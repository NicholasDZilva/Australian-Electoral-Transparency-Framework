## The Problem: Structural Asymmetry

Modern elections are shaped by three vectors of influence that operate outside public visibility:
1.  **Algorithmic Influence:** Digital platforms amplify political content without transparency.
2.  **Financial Influence:** Campaign funding is fragmented across parties and third parties.
3.  **Advocacy Ecosystems:** Organised groups campaign alongside parties without alignment disclosure.

This creates a structural advantage for entrenched players and undermines informed voter consent.

## The Solution: A Unified Transparency Layer

This framework integrates three complementary reforms into a single, coherent system:

| Vector of Influence | Reform | Delivery Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Algorithmic** | Digital Electoral Transparency | **Algorithmic Influence Dashboard** (AEC) |
| **Financial** | Voter-Directed Funding & Transparency | **Funding Transparency Dashboard** (AEC) |
| **Advocacy** | Invisible Influence (Alignment Disclosure) | **Advocacy Ecosystem Dashboard** (AEC) |

### Core Architecture
All three components share the same design principles:
-   **Public-Facing Dashboards:** The primary voter interface.
-   **Disclosure, Not Restriction:** Regulates visibility, not speech.
-   **AEC Administration:** Leverages existing, trusted infrastructure.
-   **Audit-Based Enforcement:** Focuses on compliance after the fact.
